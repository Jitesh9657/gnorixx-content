One of the biggest frustrations for new YouTube creators is publishing videos that nobody discovers. You spend hours researching. You write a script. You record and edit. You create a thumbnail. Then the video gets 12 views. Many creators assume the content is bad. Sometimes that's true.

But often the problem starts much earlier. The topic itself had little demand. Or the video targeted the wrong keyword. This is where keyword research becomes important.

Understanding what people are already searching for can dramatically increase your chances of getting discovered.

In this guide, you'll learn:

* What YouTube keywords actually are
* Why keyword research matters
* How creators find keyword opportunities
* Common mistakes to avoid
* Free ways to discover content ideas
* How keyword tools can speed up the process

## What Are YouTube Keywords?

A YouTube keyword is simply a search phrase someone types into YouTube.

Examples include:

* how to start a faceless youtube channel
* best ai tools for creators
* youtube shorts growth strategy
* script writing tips for youtube

These phrases help YouTube understand:

* What your video is about
* Which audience should see it
* When it should appear in search results
* What related videos it belongs to

Without clear keyword targeting, YouTube has a harder time understanding your content.

---

## Why Keywords Matter For Small Channels

Large channels often receive views from subscribers. Small channels usually do not. This means discoverability becomes extremely important.

Keyword research helps creators:

* Find topics people already want
* Understand audience interests
* Create relevant content
* Generate video ideas faster
* Improve search visibility

Instead of guessing what viewers want, you're using actual search behavior.

---

## Search Intent Is More Important Than Volume

Many creators obsess over search volume. Volume matters. But intent matters more.

For example:

### Keyword A

"youtube"

Massive search volume.

Almost impossible to rank.

---

### Keyword B

"how to write youtube shorts scripts"

Lower search volume. Higher relevance. Much easier to target. The second keyword is often more valuable for smaller creators because it attracts a specific audience. Specific audiences convert better.

## Understanding Different Types Of Keywords

### Informational Keywords

People want answers.

Examples:

* how to grow on youtube
* how youtube shorts work
* how to write viral hooks

These are excellent blog and video topics.

---

### Tutorial Keywords

People want step-by-step guidance.

Examples:

* how to edit youtube shorts
* how to create faceless videos
* how to generate youtube scripts

Tutorial content performs consistently well.

---

### Comparison Keywords

People are evaluating options.

Examples:

* chatgpt vs script writer
* youtube shorts vs reels
* vidiq vs tubebuddy

Comparison content often attracts highly engaged viewers.

---

### Problem-Solving Keywords

These keywords target pain points.

Examples:

* why my youtube shorts get no views
* why retention drops
* why videos stop getting impressions

Problem-focused content usually generates strong engagement because viewers are actively looking for solutions.

## How To Find Keyword Ideas For Free

Many creators believe keyword research requires expensive software. That is not true. There are several free methods available.

### YouTube Search Suggestions

Start typing a topic into YouTube search. YouTube automatically suggests popular searches.

For example:

Type:

"youtube shorts"

Suggestions may include:

* youtube shorts algorithm
* youtube shorts ideas
* youtube shorts monetization
* youtube shorts growth

These suggestions often come directly from real user searches.

---

### Related Searches

Scroll to related searches.

These can reveal:

* new content ideas
* supporting topics
* long-tail opportunities

Many creators completely ignore this section.

---

### Comment Sections

Comments reveal what people still want to learn.

Look for questions such as:

* Can you make a video about this?
* What happens if...?
* Does this work for beginners?

Every question can become a future video.

---

### Competitor Channels

Study channels within your niche.

Look for:

* recurring topics
* popular formats
* frequently discussed problems

Do not copy. Instead, identify patterns.

---

## What Are Long-Tail Keywords?

Long-tail keywords are longer, more specific search phrases.

Example:

Broad keyword:

"youtube scripts"

Long-tail keyword:

"how to write youtube shorts scripts for beginners"

Long-tail keywords often have:

* lower competition
* clearer intent
* higher conversion potential

Small creators should focus heavily on long-tail opportunities.

## Common Keyword Research Mistakes

### Targeting Extremely Broad Terms

Trying to rank for:

* youtube
* shorts
* creator

is usually unrealistic. Specific keywords perform better.

---

### Ignoring Audience Intent

A keyword may have traffic but attract the wrong audience.

Always ask:

What does this person actually want?

---

### Chasing Trends Only

Trending topics can work. But evergreen content often provides traffic for months or years. A healthy content strategy includes both.

---

### Creating Content Without Research

Many creators choose topics based purely on intuition. Research helps reduce guesswork.

---

## Building A Content System Around Keywords

Instead of finding one keyword at a time, build clusters.

Example:

### Main Topic

YouTube Shorts

Supporting Topics:

* youtube shorts hooks
* youtube shorts script templates
* youtube shorts retention
* youtube shorts mistakes
* youtube shorts ideas

One keyword often leads to dozens of related content opportunities. This approach creates topical authority over time.

## How Keyword Research Helps Script Writing

Good keywords improve more than discoverability. They improve scripting.

When you understand what viewers are searching for, you can:

* write stronger hooks
* address audience pain points
* answer specific questions
* improve retention

For example:

Instead of:

> Here's a tip about YouTube.

You can write:

> Most creators searching for YouTube Shorts growth are making this mistake.

Specificity creates relevance. Relevance improves retention.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How many keywords should I target per video?

One primary keyword and a few closely related variations are usually enough.

### Do YouTube keywords still matter?

Yes. They help YouTube understand content and connect it with relevant viewers.

### Should small creators target low-competition keywords?

Generally yes. Long-tail keywords are often easier to rank for and attract more targeted audiences.

### Are keyword tools necessary?

Not always, but they can save time and uncover opportunities you might miss manually.

### How often should I do keyword research?

Most creators benefit from researching topics before creating each new piece of content.
